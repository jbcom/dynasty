# Founding-Generation America (c. 1765–1800) — grounded research

Source material for the founding-spine onboarding + content. The player FOUNDS a
family line in the revolutionary/founding generation. Every load-bearing fact is
cited; myth-vs-scholarship flags are inline. Gathered via live web research
(WebSearch/WebFetch) across five sub-agents, reconciled with an independent
power-base report; the headright-fraud and entail/primogeniture-abolition facts
were additionally hand-verified. Supersedes the memory-only draft flagged in
[[research-not-memory]]. Pairs with `2026-06-22-founding-spine-redesign.md`.

## Anchor numbers

- 13 colonies/states ≈ **2.5M people in 1776** (~2.17M in 1770; 3.9M at 1790 census).
- **~90% of free people farmed**; ~95% rural/small-town.
- African Americans **~20%** of total, the great majority enslaved (1770 ≈18%, 1790 ≈19% — round it).
  - Regional enslaved share c.1770s: **South ~31%** (VA ~40%, SC ~60%); **Mid ~6%**; **New England ~2–3%** overall but ~20–25% in Boston/Newport.

## 1. Classes (top → bottom)

- **Landed gentry / great planters** — top ~5–10% of wealth; land = a share of political power. Southerners richest (~2× New England / Mid-Atlantic average). Gentry rise enabled by the late-17thc shift indentured→enslaved labor (slavery needed more capital than small planters had).
- **Merchants (urban commercial elite)** — atop the ports: Philadelphia ~40k, NY ~25k, Boston ~16k, Charleston ~12k. No banks → the great merchant *was* the bank (book credit, insurance, personal notes).
- **Professionals (lawyers, doctors, ministers)** — learned class ranking with the gentry. Lawyers despised early but **by the Revolution the leading men in every town** + dominant path into politics. Ministers especially authoritative in New England.
- **Artisans / tradesmen ("middling sort," "mechanics")** — skilled craftsmen, comfortable, below merchants. Apprenticeship-trained; **no English-style guilds** in the colonies.
- **Yeoman farmers (freeholders)** — numerical backbone (~60–65% of whites in some colonies). Land = the most important wealth, esp. New England.
- **Tenant farmers** — landless but free, renting from large owners; big in NY's manorial Hudson Valley.
- **Indentured servants** — the **majority of European immigrants** (½–¾). 4–7yr contracts (commonly 4–5) for passage → "freedom dues." Unfree but **NOT chattel** — kept personhood, finite terms, went free. ~40% survived their terms in the early Chesapeake.
- **Free laborers / urban poor / mariners** — wage workers, sailors, the propertyless.
- **The enslaved** — ~20% overall; labor + the single most valuable form of property, self-reproducing.

Wealth: top 1% ≈ **9.3% of income** in 1774 (Jones probate study) — real but more moderate than today's ~20%. *Wealth (stock) more concentrated than income (flow)*; Jones counts enslaved people as owners' property, skewing Southern aggregates.

**Myth flags:** egalitarian-yeoman image is idealized (farmers were profit-seekers); "indentured = slavery"/"Irish slaves" is a modern myth; rags-to-riches mobility was *rare* (self-perpetuating elite); the log cabin came from Finnish/Swedish New Sweden, not early English colonists.

## 2. Trades by tier (apprentice → journeyman → master = shop-owner, NOT most-skilled)

- **Elite/professional:** merchant/ship owner · lawyer (read law) · minister · physician (apprentice-trained) · planter (managerial) · magistrate/officeholder (a *status*, not a paid job).
- **Skilled trades ("mechanic"):** **printer** (highest mobility — Franklin's ladder) · blacksmith · silversmith (capital-heavy — Revere) · gunsmith · cooper · cabinetmaker · wheelwright · cordwainer (shoemaker) · tailor · hatter · tanner · brewer · baker · apothecary · wigmaker (collapsed when wigs left fashion ~Revolution) · miller (rural hub).
- **Maritime:** ship's captain · sailor · fisherman (cod, a top export) · whaler · **privateer** (Letters of Marque from 1775 — wartime fast fortune).
- **Agricultural:** yeoman · tenant · **overseer** (landless white managing enslaved labor — a step toward owning land/slaves) · drover.
- **Laboring/unfree:** indentured servant · day laborer · enslaved field laborer · **skilled enslaved artisan** (smith/cooper/carpenter — "trade" and "unfree" not exclusive).
- **Frontier/specialized:** **land surveyor** (lucrative £100+/yr → pipeline to land speculation; Washington's path) · fur trapper · Indian trader · tavern-keeper (a common *widow's* trade) · peddler.

Ladders to model: printing→Franklin; law→the Revolution-era political class; surveying→speculation→gentry (Washington); planter/merchant→office by default. Mobility was real but **capital-gated**.

**Myth flags:** "apprenticeships lasted 7 years" — mostly false (4yr common in labor-scarce America); "no women in trades" — **widows routinely ran husbands' businesses** (printing, shoemaking, tavern-keeping, smithing; *femme sole trader* statutes); "the village blacksmith" is a 19thc romantic invention.

## 3. Regions

- **New England (MA/CT/RI/NH):** Puritan/Congregational *established* church; town-meeting governance; rocky-soil family farms + fishing/whaling/shipbuilding; merchant elite Boston/Salem/Newport; education (Harvard 1636, Yale); few enslaved. RI the tolerant outlier.
- **Mid-Atlantic (NY/NJ/PA/DE):** the **Breadbasket** (wheat/corn); **most diverse** (English, Dutch, German, Scots-Irish, Swedes; many faiths); Quaker PA a refuge; Philadelphia + NY the lead ports; **NY patroon/manor estates** = rentier landlordism (unlike freeholding norm).
- **South (MD/VA/Carolinas/GA):** plantation staples — **tobacco** (Chesapeake), **rice & indigo** (lowcountry); NC small/medium farms; enslaved labor central; **Anglican establishment**; small gentry runs Council/Burgesses/vestries/county courts; dispersed riverine settlement.
- **Tidewater vs. backcountry** — first-order internal split: coastal Anglican gentry on slave plantations vs. post-1730 Scots-Irish + German backcountry (small farms, dissenters, under-represented in eastern assemblies; Regulator movements, NC Alamance 1771).

**Fischer's Four Folkways (*Albion's Seed*, 1989):** East Anglian Puritan→New England; South-of-England Cavalier→VA tidewater; North-Midlands Quaker→Delaware Valley; North-Britain borderlands→backcountry. **CONTESTED scholarship — flavor lens, not authoritative ethnography** (weak linguistics, static folk-culture, over-privileges British origins).

## 4. Power bases (interlocking + convertible; LAND is the substrate)

1. **LAND** — income, rank, *and the vote* (freehold suffrage; e.g. DE 50ac or £40). Headright (50ac/imported person, stackable, enslaved counted; fraud-riddled). Western speculation + **military bounty grants** = Washington (surveyor → 52,194 acres by 1800). NY patroon estates (Rensselaerswyck ~1M ac). **Entail & primogeniture** = dynastic locks; Jefferson abolished VA entail **1776**, primogeniture **1785**. *Saga lever: a family before 1776 can engineer a perpetual entailed seat; one rising after 1785 in VA sees estates legally fragment each generation.*
2. **COMMERCE** — the mercantile house (warehouse+retail+finance+ship); merchant = the bank. House of Hancock (merchant→politician; 1768 *Liberty* seizure→riot). RI dominated the mainland slave trade (~934 voyages, Newport "rum capital"). Brown family of Providence (chandlery→cartel→1764 *Sally*→Brown University; slave-trading John vs. abolitionist Moses). Smuggling + the Sugar Act (1764) → constitutional grievance. *Myth: Hancock "king of smugglers" is unfounded — most trade legit.*
3. **THE PULPIT** — religion a tax-funded arm of local government (Anglican South; Congregational New England "Standing Order"). Minister often the only college man; the 2–4hr weekly sermon "the only regular medium of public communication"; election sermons + jeremiads carried political weight. **First Great Awakening** (1730s–40s): trans-colonial celebrity, **New Lights vs Old Lights** (Whitefield reached 75–80%). Dissent → Jefferson's VA Statute for Religious Freedom 1786; Revolution's "Black Robe Regiment." *Era trend was DISESTABLISHMENT (7 states 1776–96; MA establishment fell only 1833).*
4. **LAW & POLITICS** — no law schools (Litchfield first, 1773); "**read law**" (apprenticeship + Coke/Blackstone + exam by judges). The bar = the escalator into revolutionary leadership (Jefferson under Wythe; Henry's Parsons' Cause→Burgesses in months). House of Burgesses (1619). County govt = appointed unpaid **gentry JPs**, self-perpetuating (justices nominated their successors). *By 1660 five interrelated families held every Council seat — descendants still held them in 1775 (FFV).* **Honest picture: a self-recruiting oligarchy.**
5. **THE PRESS** — printer = printer+bookseller+publisher; ~75% ran a weekly; the **postmastership** the prize sideline. 16 papers by 1740, ~37 by 1775. **Franklin** the printer-statesman. **Pamphlets** the killer app (*Common Sense*, Jan 1776, ~25 editions/yr). **Committees of Correspondence** (Boston 1772 → 12 of 13 colonies 1773). **Zenger (1735)** shifted opinion but set NO legal precedent (truth no defense to seditious libel until ~1798). *Myth: Franklin's "self-made" overstates it (patronage + partners); Common Sense sales disputed (Paine claimed 120k, skeptics ~75k).*
6. **THE MILITARY** — a military title *was* a social title (militia colonelcy "distinguished the gentry," often status w/o command). Continental Army: "an officer was a gentleman." **Society of the Cincinnati** (1783, hereditary via primogeniture) → fears of an American nobility. Service→office = the Washington template (recurs: Jackson, Harrison, Taylor, Grant). *Myth: Washington NOT self-made by the sword — rank largely inherited + MARRIED (Martha Custis's ~17,500ac estate lifted him into the upper gentry).*

**Cross-cutting corrective (the load-bearing design note):** founding-era power was a **self-recruiting oligarchy of interrelated families, land as substrate**. The rags-to-riches / log-cabin / self-made-man narrative is largely 19thc mythology — deploy it as **in-world propaganda your dynasty cultivates**, NOT the actual advancement mechanic.

## 5. Sensory details by tier (for prose grounding)

- **Planter/gentry:** dinner in removes (boiled pork, goose, roast beef, mutton, hominy, pickles → mince pies, tarts, cheese → port, **Madeira**, nuts); **liveried enslaved waiters** in heraldic colors; light as wealth-display (gilt, mirrors, 400+ candles in a ballroom; clean bright **spermaceti** vs. smoky tallow). *The mansion was NOT cozy — drafty, dim, disease still reached the rich; refinement was an effortful performance.*
- **Merchant:** the **coffeehouse was the office** (coffee, tobacco smoke, ale, ink, currency talk); the **counting house + wharf** (tide-smell, tar, rigging, crates, ledgers).
- **Artisan/middling:** shop and home one structure (apprentice boys boarding); the **leather apron** the badge; forge-glow, coal smoke, ringing hammer; pewter not silver, cider, salt pork, linsey-woolsey, rushlight.
- **Yeoman:** **hearth-centered** (cooking, spinning, candle/soap-making by the fire; constant wood smoke); labor split by sex; **root cellar + smokehouse** preservation; one/two-room house, shared beds, chamber pots, animals near, dawn-to-dusk seasonal rhythm.
- **Laborer/indentured/poor:** near-slavery but time-limited; brutal labor, thin diet, high death rate; coarse osnaburg/canvas, cornmeal-and-scraps, crowded rented rooms, cold, disease.
- **The enslaved (factual, humane):** labor sunup-to-sundown six days; one-room log cabins ~225 sqft, earthen floors, up to eight to a room; **rations measured, never enough** (Mount Vernon: 1qt cornmeal + 5–8oz fish/worker); they **fed themselves** (gardens, poultry, hunting, foraging) sustaining kinship; **osnaburg, one suit a year, one pair of shoes.** *The single most load-bearing image: the same household held people in livery and people in one annual osnaburg suit, both serving the §A dinner table.*

**Myth flags:** "they never bathed" is exaggerated (daily sponge baths; *clean linen* was the measure); the "cozy colonial" myth — even the gentry house was drafty/smoky/dim, and every tier's comfort rested on the labor of the tiers below.

## Design takeaways

1. Model the six power bases as **interlocking + convertible** with **land as substrate**, not parallel silos.
2. The founding generation was a **self-recruiting oligarchy of interlinked families** — the self-made/log-cabin myth is in-world propaganda, not the advancement mechanic.
3. Entail/primogeniture abolition (VA 1776/1785) is a concrete, datable lever (perpetual seat before 1776 vs. fragmenting estate after 1785).
4. Every tier above the enslaved rested visibly on enslaved/unfree labor — the dinner-table-and-osnaburg contrast is the most load-bearing sensory image.
